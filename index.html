Topic 1: Suspicious emails and links

Ever noticed a suspicious or strange link that maybe has cloudfront.net or j.gs in it?

These links are used by hackers or scammers to infect you with malware and steal data.

Take this email for example:


From: norepl@apple.com

Hi, 2 packs of airpods are being shipped to 6 Crook Ave, Miami, Florida. Please call 1-800-EXA-MPLE for additional shipping info

If you did not order this and you think someone has found out your password, please refer to this link: dkpg.cloudfront.net/dj3ruu9/id=649362758/appcasm/


You can see that is shows "norpl" instead of "noreply" at the start. This link also has cloudfront.net inside of it which can be dangerous if used by hackers.

These types of links can infect you computer with trojans, malware, viruses, URL:Blacklist, URL:Scam, and URL:Phising. You should now be able to identify the links now.

If you however encounter a url shortener like TinyURL, Ow.ly, or Bitly; be careful and be sure to inspect the link before clicking.

Topic 4: All Hacking Techniques

Say your a newbie, you'll probably wonder how hackers get ahold of passwords or how they get access to your computer.

Well you see, when you enter a password on a website, the site [USED] to store the password in something called a hash.

Now, a hash is basically a key or token like ndq3u4890732y8756E. For example, if your password is Password123; it may look like this: 89oyou8y6757ERT8GI2Y7676T&^rt^

So when you enter your password on the same website, it checks if the entered password matches the hash. If it does, you're in! If not, you have to enter it again.

So enough with passwords, let's talk about malware injection now. Malware injection is when you click a link or button maybe and it executes dangerous code to your opearting system

This code will either steal your files and data, delete your files without looking, or allow a hacker remote access to your computer.

So, if you ever see a suspicious ad or run into a popup that says "Your Adobe Flash Player Is Out Of Date!" just don't... don't fall for it.

Even Adware like cloudfront.net or Win32:Adware [Sig] as well as todhamilton.pro, they'll just display popup ads infected with malware so they can harm your device.
